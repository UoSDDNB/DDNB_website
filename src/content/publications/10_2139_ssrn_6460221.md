abstract: "Ribosome profiling and proteomics have revealed thousands of small open reading frame (smORF)-encoded microproteinss (MP) in the human proteome, yet their functions remain largely uncharacterized. Functional characterization is challenging due to a lack of prior knowledge about a MP’s function, highlighting the importance of predictive prioritization. To address this gap, we developed smORFpro, a public resource integrating physicochemical, genomic, transcriptomic, and translatomic features to predict MP localization, stability, and biological roles.  Global analyses of localization and functional predictions confirm our previous anecdotal observations that micrproteins are enriched in metabolic functions in the mitochondria, motivating us to prioritize functional discovery for mitochondrial MPs. To this end, we conducted a pooled CRISPR/Cas9 screen of 2,506 smORFs, identifying 112 MPs that influence growth under conditions that require optimal mitochondrial activity. Triaging these candidates according to smORFpro predictive filters enabled a validation rate of 68% for bona fide stable, mitochondrial MPs, representing a 8.5-fold improvement compared to previous screens conducted without predictive prioritization. Among these, we characterize MINA (Microprotein from Noncanonical ARHGEF9), a highly conserved 59-aa uORF-derived protein that associates with the SEL1L-HRD1 ER-associated degradation (ERAD) machinery to support mitochondrial function and metabolic stress resilience. Together, our integrative approach accelerates functional annotation, systematic prioritization, and mechanistic discovery in the microproteome, greatly enhancing the identification of mitochondrial regulators vital for cellular metabolism and stress adaptation."
